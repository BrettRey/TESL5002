Auxiliary verbs

There is a small sub-category of verbs called the auxiliary verbs. They have the NICER properties.

    N   Negation with not       Lee will not eat apples.   *Kim eats not apples.
    I    Inversion in questions       Lee has eaten apples → Has Lee eaten apples?   *Eats Lee apples?
    C   Contracted negative form       didn’t, shouldn’t   *eatn’t, *keptn’t
    E   Ellipsis       Kim was eating, and Lee was ___, too.   *Kim kept eating, and Lee kept ___, too.
    R   Rebuttal       I did tóo/só read it. (did not!)   *I tried tóo/só to read it. (*tried not!)

These include the modal auxiliaries:

    will   won't   would   wouldn't
    shall   shan't   should   shouldn't
    may   --   might   mightn't
    can   can't   could   couldn't
    must   mustn't   --   --

And a few others

    be (for passive and progressive), do (for questions), and have (for perfect)
    need, dare (not for North American English)
